# Commercial Analytics Case Study

## Business Question

How did smoking behavior change across Japanese prefectures between 2012 and 2016?

## Data Source

- Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare
- National Health and Nutrition Survey
- 2012 / 2016 Prefecture-Level Smoking Statistics

## Key Findings

- National smoking rates generally declined
- Regional variation exceeded 15 percentage points
- Gifu, Okayama, and Fukuoka showed the strongest declines
- Gunma, Yamanashi, and Fukui showed increases despite the national trend

## Business Implication

Consumer transition speed differs substantially across regions.

A one-size-fits-all strategy may not be effective.

Regional portfolio allocation and marketing investment should consider local behavioral trends.

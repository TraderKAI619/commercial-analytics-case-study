Insight 1
Significant regional differences exist across Japan.

The highest-smoking prefecture was Fukushima (39.7%), while the lowest was Kanagawa (24.99%).

This represents a gap of approximately 15 percentage points, suggesting that regional characteristics may have a meaningful impact on consumer behavior.

Insight 2
Higher smoking rates appear to be concentrated in northern and regional prefectures, while lower rates are observed in major metropolitan areas.

This may suggest that urbanization, demographic structure, lifestyle differences, or public health awareness could influence smoking behavior.

Insight 1
Significant regional differences exist across Japan.

The highest-smoking prefecture was Fukushima (39.7%), while the lowest was Kanagawa (24.99%).

This represents a gap of approximately 15 percentage points, suggesting that regional characteristics may have a meaningful impact on consumer behavior.

Insight 2
Higher smoking rates appear to be concentrated in northern and regional prefectures, while lower rates are observed in major metropolitan areas.

This may suggest that urbanization, demographic structure, lifestyle differences, or public health awareness could influence smoking behavior. 

# Key Findings

## Insight 1

Smoking rates declined nationally between 2012 and 2016.

## Insight 2

The magnitude of decline varied significantly by prefecture.

Top decline regions:

- Gifu (-11.6pp)
- Okayama (-10.9pp)
- Fukuoka (-10.0pp)

## Insight 3

Several prefectures showed increasing smoking rates despite the national decline.

Top increase regions:

- Gunma (+4.3pp)
- Yamanashi (+4.0pp)
- Fukui (+3.4pp)

## Business Implication

Consumer transition speed differs substantially across regions.

A one-size-fits-all strategy may not be effective.

Regional portfolio allocation and marketing investment should consider local behavioral trends.
